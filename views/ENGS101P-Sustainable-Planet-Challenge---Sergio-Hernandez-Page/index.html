Introduction

This is the page where I will describe the process of design of the solution for the proposed problem regarding the lack of renewable energy resources in China, focusing on the Yunnan Province.

My country research

China is the most populated country in the world, and up to 50% of its inhabitants live in rural areas.  Many of these places don't have industrial developed economies and base their household income on agriculture, tourism and the exportation of resources.

Economy 

The region of Yunnan focuses its agricultural sector on the production and exportation of tobacco and the growing local industries of tea, coffee and flowers.  Tourism is one of the fastest growing sectors of the province, essentially due to the subtropical climate it has, leading to spectacular landscapes, and an ethnically diverse population which increases the domestic tourism.  The most important natural resources in Yunnan are zinc and mainly coal, being this a major aspect of its economy to consider for our ideas on this topic.  However, we will focus on renewable and "green" technologies to tackle the problems and goals of the project.

The nominal GDP per capita in Yunnan is approximately £2750 (in accordance to this Wikipedia page, and the information published by Deutsche Bank Research and China.org.cn), which presents a tight budget to our project in terms of market prices, and therefore, in production costs.

Green Development

A major aspect in Yunnan's policies is the "green" status they give to its economy and industrial and domestic activities.  This not only contributes to the preservation of the region and the world's environment, but also protects the landscapes which attract annually millions of tourists around the world.  As we could see in the presentation given to us in our second workshop, renewable energies, such as solar panels, were impressively implemented even in poor villages. This sets our main goal (the development of a sustainable technology to improve the lives of the rural areas' inhabitants) to fit into the region's "green progress" idea.

Climate

The predominant climate of Yunnan is subtropical and stable throughout the year, although we may encounter variations in temperature during the night and day and between northern and southern regions, because there is a meaningful difference in terrain altitude, being the north over 6 km above the south, creating great variations from distant areas. Nonetheless, these temperatures generally range from 8oC during the winter up to 30oC in the summers, which is one main contributors of the reliability on tourism in the province.
