Integrated Engineering Program

I am originally from Spain and I have many friends studying in universities there, thus I have "third-person" experience of engineering degrees outside of UCL.  One of the bad aspects about technical or engineering courses in many universities is that they do not encourage the students to engage with real problems based on real situations.  This is not only a wrong approach to engineering, but it also breaks the initial motivation and excitement of students apart.  However, in UCL, and thanks to the Integrated Engineering Program and the laboratory sessions, we are able to experience real engineering from the first day, and, in my case (and probably the case of many others), that drives my willing and eagerness to learn to the highest level.

My country and challenge

I have been allocated into the China group, and for me this constitutes a difficult but exiting challenge since I have not been in the country, ergo I do not know much about its culture, economy, climate, resources and other important information relevant to the design process.

Between the two challenges, we have decided to find a solution for Challenge 1, which asks us to design a stove for domestic multipurpose usage, taking into account the cost (both initial and maintenance), a high sustainability, effectiveness and safety levels, and a simple design and interface.
